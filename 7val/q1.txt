Objective: Given an integer n, your task is to convert it to binary and then find the maximum number of consecutive 
1's in its binary representation.

Input:

The input consists of a single integer n, where 1 <= n <= 10^6.
Output:

Return a single integer representing the maximum number of consecutive 1's in the binary representation of n.
Example:

Input: 13

Output: 2

Explanation: The binary representation of 13 is 1101, which has a maximum of two consecutive 1s.
